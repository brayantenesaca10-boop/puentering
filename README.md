# Puente

## A Visibility Company

Every person has a story worth knowing. The world is structured to make sure nobody hears it. We're changing that.

Puente is not a ring company. Puente is not a tech company. Puente is a visibility company. The ring is the first product. It won't be the last.

## The Belief

The janitor at your school coached soccer for 20 years. The woman who does hair on your block raised two boys alone and sends money home every month. The receptionist at your church immigrated from Ecuador, earned her CPA, and bought her own house. They are right there. Every day. And nobody knows.

Not because their stories aren't worth telling. Because nobody ever made space to hear them.

Puente makes that space.

## The Energy

This is not a product built from need. This is a product built from confidence.

Wrong energy: "Please see me. I'm invisible. I need this."
Right energy: "I know who I am. When you tap this ring, you're going to find out too."

The ring wearer is not asking for something. They are offering something. That is generosity. That is power.

## The Enemy

Invisibility. Not isolation. Invisibility.

Isolation means "I'm alone." Invisibility means "I'm here but nobody sees me."

## The Product

### The Ring

A black zirconia ceramic ring with an NFC chip sealed inside. No screen. No battery. No charging. Looks like jewelry. Works like a bridge.

Tap it on any phone. Your story appears.

### The Experience (This Is the Actual Product)

1. You get your ring. You open the app.
2. The app asks you five questions, one at a time, out loud:
   - "Say your name and where you're from."
   - "What do you do that people don't know about?"
   - "What's the meal that reminds you of home?"
   - "What could you talk about for an hour without getting bored?"
   - "What's something people are always surprised to learn about you?"
3. Each answer is 15 seconds of your voice. The app stitches them into your profile.
4. AI clones your voice and translates your story into any language. Not a robot. You.
5. You tap your ring on someone's phone. Your profile opens - your story, your voice, in their language.
6. They see a button: "Connect with [your name]"
7. They enter their first name, email, phone.
8. AI cross-references everything to find what connects you. Same hometown. Same interests. Same church. Kids at the same school.
9. Both of you see the connection - what you share, why you should talk.
10. The app shows your visibility map over time - every person who now knows your story.

The magic is at steps 8 and 9. That is the demo. That is the Macintosh moment. Two strangers tap and instantly see why they belong in each other's lives.

The ring is the hardware. The five questions are the onboarding. The moment of connection is the product.

### Why the Five Questions Matter

"Tell your story" is a blank page. Nobody does it. It feels like homework.

Five small questions that take 2 minutes build a story without the person realizing it. They never faced a blank page. They answered questions that felt easy. But when they hear their own voice played back as a profile, they think "that's me." That's the activation moment.

The first question - "say your name and where you're from" - is the hook. Everyone can do it. Once they hear their own voice, they answer the next four.

## Marketing & Positioning

### The Core Principle

The product IS the marketing. Every tap is a demo and a sales channel. Traditional marketing (ads, social campaigns, influencer deals) is secondary. The primary growth engine is the experience itself.

### Positioning

Puente does not exist in any current category. It is not a smart ring. It is not a social network. It is not a business card. It is a new category: personal visibility.

We do not compete with Ping, Popl, or any NFC product. They are in the "digital business card" category. We are in the "your story matters" category. Different shelf. Different buyer. Different emotion.

### The Marketing Funnel

The funnel is not: ad > landing page > purchase.
The funnel is: someone taps a ring > you experience the moment > you want one.

1. A Puente user taps their ring on your phone
2. You see their story, hear their voice, discover what connects you
3. You think "I want people to know MY story like that"
4. You buy through their referral link on puentering.com
5. You become the next node. You tap. Someone else wants one.

Every ring holder is a walking billboard and a sales rep. They don't know it and they don't need to. The experience does the selling.

### Content Strategy: Film the Moment

The most powerful marketing asset Puente will ever have is a real video of a real person tapping their ring for the first time and watching someone discover their story.

Phase 1: Pre-launch content
- Film yourself going to barbershops, churches, soccer fields
- Ask people: "what's something nobody around here knows about you?"
- Capture the reactions. The surprise. The emotion.
- This is pre-product content that builds the narrative before the ring ships
- Post on TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
- No mention of the product yet. Just human stories. Build the audience first.

Phase 2: Launch content
- Film the strategic 10 using their rings for the first time
- Go to their place of work. The school. The barbershop. The church.
- Record the moment someone taps the ring and sees the story
- Record the moment two people see what connects them
- This is the Macintosh demo. Real. Unscripted. Raw.
- These videos are the ads. No production needed. The authenticity IS the production value.

Phase 3: User-generated content
- Ring holders naturally create moments every time they tap
- Encourage them to film and share their connection moments
- "I just found out my neighbor and I are from the same town in Ecuador"
- The content creates itself because the experience is worth sharing

### The Anti-Marketing Playbook

Puente does not do:
- Paid ads (not at this stage - maybe never)
- Influencer partnerships (the users ARE the influencers)
- Feature comparison charts
- "Limited time offer" urgency tactics
- Discounts or sales

Puente does:
- Real stories from real people
- Film the moment, not the product
- Let the experience sell itself
- Referral through connection, not codes
- Content that makes you feel something, not content that explains something

### Voice on Social Media

Every post should make someone feel one of two things:
1. "That could be me" (recognition)
2. "I had no idea" (discovery)

Never post about the ring's features. Never post about NFC or ceramic or technology. Post about people. Always people.

Example posts:
- Video of a barber telling you he used to be an architect in Brazil. Caption: "Your barber has a story you've never heard."
- Video of two strangers discovering they're from the same town. Caption: "They've been neighbors for 3 years."
- Photo of a ring on someone's hand at a church dinner. Caption: "100 stories in one room. How many do you know?"

### Pricing as Positioning

$49 is not a price. It is a statement. It says: this is worth something. This is not a gimmick from Amazon. This is not a toy. This is a product for people who know their story matters.

We do not discount. We do not run sales. The price is the price. If someone thinks $49 is too much to tell their story and be known, they are not our customer.

## Growth Model

### Referral Through Experience

Every ring holder gets a unique profile link. When they tap their ring, the person who experiences it can buy their own ring through that link.

No invite codes. No applications. No artificial gates. Just: "I tapped someone's ring, it changed how I see them, I want one."

### Expansion Path

Batch 1: 100 rings in Danbury, CT. Churches, soccer leagues, barbershops.
Batch 2: 200-500 rings. Expand to neighboring Connecticut towns.
Batch 3: New cities with dense multicultural communities.

The pattern: find a community where people are side by side but not seen. Seed 10 rings with magnetic invisible people. Film the moments. Let it grow.

### Beyond the Ring

The ring is product one. The visibility platform is the company. Future products could include:
- Community visibility events (Puente Nights)
- Employer partnerships (help workplaces see their people)
- School programs (students sharing their backgrounds)
- The Puente app as a standalone visibility platform

But not yet. Right now: one ring, one experience, one community.
