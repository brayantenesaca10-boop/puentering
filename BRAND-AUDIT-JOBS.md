# Puente - Brand Audit (Steve Jobs Framework)

## The Three Questions

### 1. What do they feel when they tap?

Instant connection. When someone has a Puente Ring, taps it on someone else's phone, that person fills out their info, AI finds all the matches instantly. The person with the Puente ring feels heard. For maybe the first time in that building, in that neighborhood, in that job - someone sees them.

### 2. Who is the first adopter?

Janitors, teachers, receptionists. People sharing their story in a new way. The people the world has decided don't matter. The ones who are surrounded by people every day but never seen.

### 3. What is the separation you are bridging?

Invisibility. Not isolation. Invisibility.

Isolation means "I'm alone." Invisibility means "I'm here but nobody sees me." People are surrounded but not seen. That is the disease Puente cures.

## The Real Why

Brayan's mother's journey: Ecuador to the US, cleaning McDonald's bathrooms, to university, CPA license, buying her own house. No one talks about it. No one knows it. But she is dying to share it. Stories go untold. That is what Puente solves.

No other ring, social media, website, or business card does this. Puente makes invisible people visible.

## The Debate: What Puente Actually Is

If you're solving isolation, Puente is a networking tool.
If you're solving invisibility, Puente is a dignity tool.
Those are two completely different companies.

Puente is a visibility company. The ring is the first product. It won't be the last.

When that janitor taps his ring and someone sees his story, his interests, the AI shows them they both love soccer, they both came from Ecuador, they both have a daughter the same age - he didn't just "connect." He became visible. For maybe the first time in that building.

That's not a feature. That's a moment. People will forget every spec on that ring. They will never forget the first time someone actually saw them.

## Steve's Three Branding Rules

### 1. Start with the belief, not the product

Puente doesn't start with "we made an NFC ring." Puente starts with "every person has a story worth knowing, and the world is structured to make sure nobody hears it. We're changing that." The ring is just how you deliver on that belief.

### 2. Say no to almost everything

Every feature you add dilutes the message. Every option you give the customer is you admitting you don't know what the product should be. One ring. One color. One experience. That's the product. When someone sees it, they know.

### 3. The experience is the brand

Not the logo. Not the website. The moment. The moment is when someone taps their ring and for the first time, someone sees their whole story. That moment is worth more than every dollar you'll ever spend on marketing. Because that person will tell everyone. Not because you asked them to. Because they can't help it.

## The Decision Filter

Every decision from today forward runs through one filter: does this make invisible people more visible? If yes, do it. If no, cut it.
