# Puente - Brand Audit (Steve Jobs Framework)

## The Three Questions

### 1. What do they feel when they tap?

Instant connection. When someone has a Puente Ring, taps it on someone else's phone, that person fills out their info, AI finds all the matches instantly. The person with the Puente ring feels heard. For maybe the first time in that building, in that neighborhood, in that job - someone sees them.

### 2. Who is the first adopter?

Janitors, teachers, receptionists. People sharing their story in a new way. The people the world has decided don't matter. The ones who are surrounded by people every day but never seen.

### 3. What is the separation you are bridging?

Invisibility. Not isolation. Invisibility.

Isolation means "I'm alone." Invisibility means "I'm here but nobody sees me." People are surrounded but not seen. That is the disease Puente cures.

## The Real Why

Brayan's mother's journey: Ecuador to the US, cleaning McDonald's bathrooms, to university, CPA license, buying her own house. No one talks about it. No one knows it. But she is dying to share it. Stories go untold. That is what Puente solves.

No other ring, social media, website, or business card does this. Puente makes invisible people visible.

## The Debate: What Puente Actually Is

If you're solving isolation, Puente is a networking tool.
If you're solving invisibility, Puente is a dignity tool.
Those are two completely different companies.

Puente is a visibility company. The ring is the first product. It won't be the last.

When that janitor taps his ring and someone sees his story, his interests, the AI shows them they both love soccer, they both came from Ecuador, they both have a daughter the same age - he didn't just "connect." He became visible. For maybe the first time in that building.

That's not a feature. That's a moment. People will forget every spec on that ring. They will never forget the first time someone actually saw them.

## Steve's Three Branding Rules

### 1. Start with the belief, not the product

Puente doesn't start with "we made an NFC ring." Puente starts with "every person has a story worth knowing, and the world is structured to make sure nobody hears it. We're changing that." The ring is just how you deliver on that belief.

### 2. Say no to almost everything

Every feature you add dilutes the message. Every option you give the customer is you admitting you don't know what the product should be. One ring. One color. One experience. That's the product. When someone sees it, they know.

### 3. The experience is the brand

Not the logo. Not the website. The moment. The moment is when someone taps their ring and for the first time, someone sees their whole story. That moment is worth more than every dollar you'll ever spend on marketing. Because that person will tell everyone. Not because you asked them to. Because they can't help it.

## The Decision Filter

Every decision from today forward runs through one filter: does this make invisible people more visible? If yes, do it. If no, cut it.

## Product Flow (The Moment)

1. You wear the ring. You tap it on someone's phone.
2. Your profile opens - your name, where you're from, your story in your voice, in their language.
3. They see a button: "Connect with [your name]"
4. They enter: first name, email, phone.
5. AI instantly cross-references everything to find what connects you. Same hometown. Same interests. Kids at the same school. Same church.
6. Both of you see the connection - what you share, why you should talk.
7. The app shows your visibility map over time - every person who now knows your story.

The magic is at steps 5 and 6. That is the demo. That is the Macintosh moment.

## Networking vs Visibility

Puente is NOT a networking tool. Networking is transactional - what can this person do for me? Puente is about being seen for who you ARE, not what you can DO for someone. The janitor doesn't need to network. He needs to be visible.

The network effect happens naturally. When someone taps your ring and sees "you're both from Ecuador, you both play soccer" - they tell their friends. Not because of gamification. Because the experience changed how they see their neighbor.

Don't engineer the network effect. Engineer the moment. The network effect follows.

## Website Voice

The website speaks TO the person, not ABOUT them. Second person, not third person.

Wrong: "Someone you know has a story"
Right: "You have a story nobody around you knows"

The buyer IS the person who hasn't been seen. But the energy is CONFIDENCE, not need.

Wrong energy: "Please see me. I'm invisible. I need this ring."
Right energy: "I know who I am. When you tap this ring, you're going to find out too."

The ring wearer isn't asking for something. They're OFFERING something. That's not need. That's generosity. That's power.

"Be seen" implies you aren't seen and you need help. "Now they'll know" implies you've always known your worth - the ring just lets everyone else in on it.

## Onboarding: Making Story-Telling Fun

Never ask someone to "tell their story." That's a blank page. That's homework. Instead, ask five small, fun questions that BUILD their story:

1. "Say your name and where you're from." (easiest - everyone can do this)
2. "What do you do that people don't know about?"
3. "What's the meal that reminds you of home?"
4. "What could you talk about for an hour without getting bored?"
5. "What's something people are always surprised to learn about you?"

Each answer is 15 seconds of voice. The app stitches them into a profile. The person never faced a blank page. They answered five easy questions. But the result feels like a story.

The first question is the activation moment. Once they hear their own voice on the profile, they're hooked.

## Strategic Launch: The First 10

Don't give rings to the quietest people. Give them to the most MAGNETIC invisible people - the ones who are already hubs but whose stories are hidden:

- The barber who talks to 40 people a day
- The church receptionist who greets every family on Sunday
- The soccer coach who every kid loves
- The teacher everyone respects but nobody really knows

These people already have reach. They just don't have visibility. When they tap their ring and someone sees their real story, that person tells 10 others. The product sells itself through the experience.

## Growth: Referral Through Experience

Every ring holder gets a unique link. When they tap their ring, the person who experiences it can buy their own ring through that link. No invite codes. No applications. Just: "I tapped someone's ring, it changed how I see them, I want one."

The product IS the marketing. Every tap is a demo and a sales channel.
